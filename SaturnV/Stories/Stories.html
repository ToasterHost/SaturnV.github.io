Stories

Eventually the Saturn V became obsolete. Technology advanced and better, more efficient rockets were built. However, this rocket was designed by one of the most intuitive rocket designers of that time, Dr. Wernher von Braun. Originally, Braun was an inventor for the Nazis. He designed the V-2 rockets, some of the most threatening weapons of world war two. Braun had been fascinated by space travel ever since he was a boy, he taught himself the math and physics behind rocket travel at a young age. By early 1945, Dr. Braun knew the Allies were going to win so he started planning for after the war. 10

For fifteen after he surrendered to the Allies, he and his team of rocket engineers were taken to the United States, where they designed ballistic missiles for the American military. Once 1960 hit, Braun's team was moved from the army to the newly founded NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration). They were tasked to design the Saturn V rocket with Wernher von Braun as the lead architect. 10

Obviously the mission was a great success with the construction and launch of thirteen Saturn V rockets. The Apollo missions put humans farther than ever before, all thanks to this German rocket engineer.
